Why Visit Marrakech

The Magical City

Marrakech is one of the most vibrant and historically rich cities in Africa. With its blend of ancient medinas, modern infrastructure, and world-class hospitality, it is the perfect backdrop for an international conference.

The conference venue, ENSA — National School of Applied Sciences, is located within Cadi Ayyad University campus, offering modern facilities and easy city access.

Must-Visit Attractions

Djemaa el-Fna

The heart of Marrakech, a bustling square filled with street performers, food stalls, and local artisans. A UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage site.

Majorelle Garden

Relax in the stunning botanical gardens once owned by Yves Saint Laurent. A tranquil oasis of cobalt blue and exotic plants in the heart of the city.

Bahia & El Badi Palaces

Marvel at the architectural grandeur and historical significance of these palaces, showcasing centuries of Moroccan craftsmanship.

Saadian Tombs

A historical site showcasing intricate Moroccan craftsmanship from the Saadian dynasty, rediscovered in 1917.

The Souks

A labyrinth of colorful markets offering spices, textiles, handmade crafts, and traditional Moroccan goods. An unmissable cultural experience.

Koutoubia Mosque

Iconic religious landmark with beautiful Moroccan design. The tallest structure in Marrakech, built in the 12th century.
